Summary of Twelve Pillars
by
Jim Rohn and Chris Widener

Chapter 4: Achieving Your Goals
  • It is always best to write down your goals.

  • A side benefit to achieving your goals is that it will make you a better person,
    but "the major reason for setting a goal is for what it makes of you to accomplish it".

Chapter 5: The Proper Use of Time
  • “Every day has many opportunities, but only one best opportunity.” The best opportunities
    to focus on are the ones that align with your overall goals,because its better to suffer the pain of discipline than regret.

Chapter 6: Surround Yourself with the Best People
  • “Don't join an easy crowd; you won't grow. Go where the expectations and the demands to perform are high.”

  • The people you associate with can have a great influence on the type of person you are/become.
    Therefore it is always best to have pursue the company of ambitious, discipline and other
    goal-oriented individuals who possess attitudes and skills that you would like to develop.

Chapter 7: Be a Life Long Learner
  • “Formal education will make you a living. Self-education will make you a fortune.”

  • Self-education is what you teach yourself along the journey of life so that your mind is
    constantly growing.

  • Learning is the beginning of wealth and most of life is lived after your formal education, this means that it is very
    important for you to keep feeding yourself with knowledge to improve your quality of life.

Application to my Life?
  • I believe this book contains many excellent and important life long lessions that can be beneficial to everyone.
    For me chapter 7 was most thought provoking as it spoke about surrounding yourself with the best people.
    This chapter really made me pause and thing of the company I keep and examine the type of impact they may have on me,
    whether it is good or bad? And the chapter encouraged me even more to purse to be in the company of people who will make me better.
